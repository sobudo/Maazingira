Wild Boar

Where they live: Europe, Asia
Food: Fruits, seeds, roots, meat scavanegr
Type: Omnivore

The wild boar is in the same family as the domestic pig. It has a pig-like snout, and its canines are visible even when the mouth is closed. An adult wild boar has a thick, coarse hair coat that’s black to brownish-red to white. Wild boars are nocturnal, and live in groups of 6 to 20 called "sounders". A wild boar can live in any climate except in extremely cold or extremely hot habitats.

Arctic Fox

Where they live: Arctic region
Food:Rodents like lemming, fish, and birds, but it will also scavenge for carrion
Type:Carnivore

The Arctic fox lives in snow burrows in temperatures as low as minus 50 degrees centigrade in the treeless tundra of the Arctic regions. This extends into Eurasia, North America, the Canadian archipelago, the Siberian islands, Alaska, Greenland, Iceland. The Arctic fox has thick fur that in winter changes to white or blue-gray to blend in with the snow, and after winter changes back to brown or gray to blend in with the tundra’s rocks and plants. The fur enables it to maintain steady body temperature as well, and its thick paws allow it to walk on snow and ice. The Arctic fox has strong hearing thanks to its wide, frontward-facing ears which enable it to locate prey even beneath the snow. On hearing the prey’s location, it leaps into the air and breaks through the snow layers and grabs the prey hiding beneath.

Snow Leopard

Snow leopards live in colder parts of the world around the mountains of central Asia, in countries such as Russia, Mongolia, Kazakhstan, Uzbekistan, India, Nepal and Bhutan. Snow leopards are camouflaged in their mountain habitat and are rarely seen in the wild. Unlike other big cats, snow leopards are unable to roar. Snow leopards are physically the smallest members of the group of animals known as the ‘big cats’. (This group also includes lions, tigers, leopards and jaguars.) Snow leopards’ tails are longer, in proportion to body size, than those of most other cats. This may help to provide them with better balance for walking in rocky environments. Snow leopards have smoky-grey coloured fur, which provides camouflage in rocky, snow-covered habitat. The snow leopard’s spots – known as rosettes – help it to blend in with the rocks on the slopes of the mountains. Whereas most big cats have yellow eyes, snow leopards have light green or grey eyes. This helps them to blend into their environment. Snow leopards have thick fur. Their tails, which are not only long, but also thick, are used to protect their faces from the cold when the animal is resting. Snow leopards have wide paws, which allow them to walk on snow without sinking into it.

Bactrian Camel

The Bactrian camel has two humps used for storing fat. They use the fat by turning it into energy so they can go for a long time without food or water. When the humps on a Bactrian camel are full of fat they stand upright. When their fat reserves are low then the humps become droopy. They have a thick fur coat to keep them warm in the winter. Bactrian camels are actually critically endangered. The biggest threat to them is humans destroying their natural habitat for farming and mining. They are mainly found in Mongolia and China. Bactrian camels have only one natural predator - the grey wolf. They are the only land mammals capable of drinking salt water without any ill effects. They have sealable nostrils, to stop the sand getting up their nose.
