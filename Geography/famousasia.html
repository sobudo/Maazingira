Giant Pandas

Where they live: China
Food: Bamboo
Type: Herbivores

Giant pandas (often referred to as simply “pandas”) are black and white bears.
In the wild, they are found in thick bamboo forests, high up in the mountains of central China.
These magnificent mammals are omnivores.
But whilst they will occasionally eat small animals and fish, bamboo counts for 99 percent of their diet

Tigers

Where they live:China
Food:Large Mammals eg wild pigs, deer, buffalo
Type:Carnivores

Tigers are the largest wild cats in the world.
Tigers are solitary and hunt at night. They quietly stalk their prey until they are close enough to pounce – then they kill their victim with a bite to the neck or back of the head.
Tigers like water and are good swimmers.
Every tiger in the world is unique – no two tigers have the same pattern of stripes.

Asian Elephant

The Asian elephant is smaller than the African elephant, and has smaller ears.
The Indian elephant’s tough skin is smoother and less wrinkled than that of the African elephant.
They have light patches on or around the ears and trunk.
Only male Indian elephants have tusks.
If you look closely at the tip of an Asian elephant’s trunk, you’ll see that it has a single small, finger-like bump at the top. African elephants have two of these: one at the top and one at the bottom.

Indian Rhino

Indian rhinoceros (great one-horned rhinoceros) is the second largest member (white rhino is the largest) of a rhino family. It can be found in India and Nepal. They live in floodplain grasslands, swamps and forests. They are htreatened by habitat loss due to agricultural development and poaching. Indian rhinoceros has brownish-grey skin with folds that create appearance of an armor. Shoulders and legs are covered with wart-like bumps. Indian rhinoceros has heavy skull, prehensile upper lip, flexible ears, bulky body and short tail. Indian rhinoceros has only one horn that can reach 9.8 to 22.5 inches in length. Horn is made of keratin, just like human hair and nails. Indian rhinoceros has poor eyesight, but excellent sense of hearing and smell.Despite its bulky body, Indian rhinoceros is very fast animal (maximum speed 34 miles per hour) that can quickly change direction of movement. Indian rhinoceros is grazer. Its diet is mostly based on the grass. Indian rhinoceros is very good swimmer..

Snow Leopard

Snow leopards live in colder parts of the world around the mountains of central Asia, in countries such as Russia, Mongolia, Kazakhstan, Uzbekistan, India, Nepal and Bhutan. Snow leopards are camouflaged in their mountain habitat and are rarely seen in the wild. Unlike other big cats, snow leopards are unable to roar. Snow leopards are physically the smallest members of the group of animals known as the ‘big cats’. (This group also includes lions, tigers, leopards and jaguars.) Snow leopards’ tails are longer, in proportion to body size, than those of most other cats. This may help to provide them with better balance for walking in rocky environments. Snow leopards have smoky-grey coloured fur, which provides camouflage in rocky, snow-covered habitat. The snow leopard’s spots – known as rosettes – help it to blend in with the rocks on the slopes of the mountains. Whereas most big cats have yellow eyes, snow leopards have light green or grey eyes. This helps them to blend into their environment. Snow leopards have thick fur. Their tails, which are not only long, but also thick, are used to protect their faces from the cold when the animal is resting. Snow leopards have wide paws, which allow them to walk on snow without sinking into it.

Bactrian Camel

The Bactrian camel has two humps used for storing fat. They use the fat by turning it into energy so they can go for a long time without food or water. When the humps on a Bactrian camel are full of fat they stand upright. When their fat reserves are low then the humps become droopy. They have a thick fur coat to keep them warm in the winter. Bactrian camels are actually critically endangered. The biggest threat to them is humans destroying their natural habitat for farming and mining. They are mainly found in Mongolia and China. Bactrian camels have only one natural predator - the grey wolf. They are the only land mammals capable of drinking salt water without any ill effects. They have sealable nostrils, to stop the sand getting up their nose.
