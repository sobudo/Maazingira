Butterfly life Cycle

Probably the most beautiful insect people are not afraid of! Lets explore a butterflies life from birth to adult. As they grow, they go through an incredible process called ‘metamorphisis’ To grow into an adult they go through 4 stages: egg, larva, pupa and adult. Depending on the type of butterfly, the life cycle of a butterfly may take anywhere from one month to a whole year. You can print out this Butterfly Life Cycle coloring page to follow along as we talk about the 4 stages.

Stage 1: The Egg

The female butterfly lays eggs on leaves of plants. The eggs are very small and can be round, oval
or cylindrical and smooth, bumpy or wrinkled depending on the species.
With some eggs, if you look close enough you can actually see the tiny caterpillar growing inside of it,
especially Monarch butterflies! Some eggs hatch within a few days and in others they will only hatch
once the weather is warm enough.

Stage 2: The Larva (Caterpillar)

Out of the egg hatches a caterpillar and in this stage all they do is eat. They love to eat. They actually
eat their way out of the egg and begin to eat the leaf they were born onto.
This is really important because the mother butterfly needs to lay her eggs on the type of leaf the
caterpillar will eat – each caterpillar type likes only certain types of leaves.
Caterpillars need to eat and eat so they can grow quickly.
When a caterpillar is born, they are extremely small. A fully grown caterpillar can be over 100 times
larger than when it emerged from its egg. When they start eating, they instantly start growing and
expanding.
Their skin does not stretch or grow, so they grow by “molting” (sheding the outgrown skin) several
times while it grows. They shed their skin four or five times – the skin becomes too tight and splits
open, revealing a new, larger skin underneath.

Stage 3: Pupa (Chrysalis)

The pupa stage is one of the coolest stages of a butterfly’s life. As soon as a caterpillar is done growing
and they have reached their full length/weight, they form themselves into a pupa, also known as a
chrysalis. From the outside of the pupa, it looks as if the caterpillar may just be resting, but the inside
is where all of the action is. Inside of the pupa, the caterpillar is rapidly changing. Within the chrysalis
the old body parts of the caterpillar are undergoing a remarkable transformation, called ‘metamorphosis,’
to become the beautiful parts that make up the butterfly that will emerge.

Stage 4: Adult Butterfly

Finally, when the caterpillar has done all of its forming and changing inside the pupa. When the butterfly
first emerges from the chrysalis, both of the wings are going to be soft and folded against its body. This
is because the butterfly had to fit all its new parts inside of the pupa. As soon as the butterfly has rested
after coming out of the chrysalis, it will pump blood into the wings in order to get them working and
flapping – then they get to fly. Usually within a three or four-hour period, the butterfly will master flying
and will search for a mate in order to reproduce. When in the fourth and final stage of their lives, adult
butterflies are constantly on the look out to reproduce and when a female lays their eggs on some
leaves, the butterfly life cycle will start all over.
